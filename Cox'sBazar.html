Explore Cox's Bazar: A Complete Travel Guide

As life gets monotonous with every passing day, the most incredible way to relax is to go somewhere completely different. The beach is as far away from work as feasible for those in the city. From the longest sandy beach in the world to famous traditional food and seafood, Cox's Bazar has made itself one of Bangladesh's most prominent tourist destinations. Cox's Bazar is located on the southwest coast of Bangladesh, about 150 km (90 miles) south of the city of Chattogram (Chittagong). A visit to Cox's Bazar can undoubtedly provide a break from everything that is bothering your mental growth. So don't forget to check out this blog detailing Cox's Bazar with everything you can explore while visiting Cox's Bazar.

When to Visit Cox's Bazar?

When you plan a trip to a new destination, the first thing that comes to mind is when you should go? Bangladesh is a six-season country; thus, each season offers visitors a unique perspective and beauty. Cox's Bazar grows more desirable and attractive with each passing season in Bangladesh. Summer days are ideal for beach activities, while the monsoon brings the beach of Cox's Bazar to life. Moreover, Winter is the most beautiful season, when the sun isn't scorching hot, and the weather is gloomy and cool. How to Travel to Cox's Bazar?

Cox's Bazar connects Bangladesh's central districts by road and air. If you live in Dhaka, you can fly to Cox's Bazar 50 minutes from Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport. This mode of transportation is currently the most convenient and time-saving. Furthermore, some people prefer to go by car and bus rather than by flight. Moreover, some people enjoy long road trips and frequently travel to Cox's Bazar in their vehicles. Hence, your current residing place has no impact, and you'll be able to locate a suitable mode of transportation to visit Cox's Bazar at any time.

Cox's Bazar: Attraction & Activities

At first glance, you can start with Cox's Bazar Beach to feel the sound of the waves and the breeze. You may begin walking the coast from end to end at several beach points such as Suganda Beach, Laboni Beach and Kolatoli Beach. Don't forget to check out the coral beach named Inani Beach, about 32 km from Dolphin Square. In addition, you should go on a drive along the Marine Drive Road of Cox's Bazar. This road offers the most scenic beauty that covers the beautiful beaches of Cox's Bazar and the mountains on each side.
